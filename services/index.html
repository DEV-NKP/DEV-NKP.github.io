06 / Questions

SALESFORCE FAQ

Comprehensive answers to frequently asked questions about my Salesforce development services, process, capabilities, and working style.

What Salesforce clouds do you specialize in?
I have extensive experience across all major Salesforce clouds. My core expertise covers Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, Experience Cloud, Financial Services Cloud, Health Cloud, and Nonprofit Success Pack (NPSP). I also work with Marketing Cloud, Education Cloud, and specialized solutions like Salesforce Shield for compliance-heavy industries. Each engagement is tailored to the cloud that best fits your industry requirements — I won't recommend a solution that doesn't serve your specific business context.
How do you handle data migration from legacy systems?
I follow a structured 5-phase approach: Assessment (mapping source data), Cleansing (removing duplicates, fixing inconsistencies), Mapping (aligning fields to Salesforce schema), Migration (using Data Loader, Apex Data Loader, or Import Wizard), and Validation (record counts, spot-checks, business rule verification). I have successfully migrated 10,000+ records with 99.9% accuracy. Every migration includes a rollback plan and post-migration monitoring period.
Can you work with an existing Salesforce org?
Absolutely — the majority of my engagements involve existing orgs. I begin every project with a thorough Salesforce Health Check covering metadata complexity, governor limit usage, security posture, and technical debt. From there, I provide a prioritized recommendation list. I'm comfortable navigating complex legacy configurations, third-party managed packages, and multi-sandbox environments. My goal is to enhance your existing investment, not replace what's already working.
What is your approach to custom Apex and LWC development?
I always follow a declarative-first philosophy — using Flows, validation rules, and formula fields before reaching for code. When custom code is necessary, I write bulkified Apex with proper trigger handler patterns, follow SOLID principles, maintain 80%+ test coverage (typically 90%+), and use Lightning Web Components with the latest best practices. All code is version-controlled in Git with meaningful commit messages and code review documentation, so your team can maintain it independently after handover.
Do you provide training and documentation?
Yes — training and documentation are built into every engagement, not sold as extras. I deliver role-specific training sessions (end users, power users, admins, developers), written documentation with screenshots, video recordings of walkthroughs, and Salesforce in-app help text where relevant. Training can be delivered live via Google Meet/Zoom or asynchronously. The goal is for your team to be self-sufficient and confident, not dependent on me for every change.
What security measures do you implement?
Security is architectured into every solution from day one. I implement Salesforce Shield (field-level encryption, event monitoring, Platform Encryption), role-based access control (profiles, permission sets, sharing rules), field-level security, and proper data classification. For regulated industries: HIPAA-compliant setups for healthcare, PCI-DSS configurations for payment processing, and GDPR-aligned data retention policies. I also conduct regular security scans using the Salesforce Security Health Check tool.
What is a typical project timeline?
Timelines depend heavily on scope and complexity. As a general guide: Simple configurations (custom objects, flows, reports): 1–3 weeks. Standard implementations (CRM setup with integrations): 4–8 weeks. Complex custom development (LWC apps, multi-cloud): 8–14 weeks. Enterprise-scale projects (multi-phase, migration included): 14–24 weeks. I provide a detailed project plan with milestones during the discovery phase. My agile approach ensures you see working software early and often — not just at the end.
Do you offer post-launch support?
Yes. Every project includes a 30-day hypercare period with priority bug fixes and performance monitoring at no additional cost. Beyond that, I offer flexible support arrangements: a monthly retainer for ongoing development and admin support, ad-hoc hourly support, or a fixed-scope enhancement package. Many clients maintain a retainer relationship after initial delivery because Salesforce evolves rapidly with 3 major releases per year — I help you stay current and take advantage of new features.
Do you work with international clients?
Absolutely. I work with clients in the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, and across the globe. I'm experienced with asynchronous remote collaboration, different time zone scheduling, and communication tools like Slack, Zoom, and Microsoft Teams. Based in Dhaka, Bangladesh (UTC+6), I can accommodate flexible hours to overlap with US or European business hours when needed. International payment via Wise, PayPal, or bank transfer.
